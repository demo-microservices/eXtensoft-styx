A stakeholder refers to an individual, group, or organization that has an interest or "stake" in the software being developed. Stakeholders can include various entities such as clients, customers, end-users, project managers, business owners, investors, marketing teams, legal departments, and more. They are directly or indirectly impacted by the software and have a vested interest in its success.

Creating stakeholder personas or profiles is a way to better understand and represent the different perspectives, goals, and requirements of these individuals or groups. Similar to user personas, stakeholder personas help software development teams consider the needs and expectations of various stakeholders throughout the development process.

Stakeholder personas typically include the following information:

  1. Role: The stakeholder's position or role in the organization or project.
  2. Objectives: The specific goals, objectives, or outcomes the stakeholder wants to achieve through the software.
  3. Responsibilities: The stakeholder's responsibilities and areas of influence related to the software.
  4. Communication Preferences: How the stakeholder prefers to communicate and be engaged throughout the development process.
  5. Concerns and Constraints: The stakeholder's concerns, constraints, or limitations that may impact the software development or implementation.
  6. Expectations: The stakeholder's expectations regarding functionality, usability, timeline, budget, etc.
  7. Decision-making Authority: The level of decision-making authority the stakeholder holds in relation to the software.

By creating stakeholder personas, development teams can gain insights into the unique perspectives and requirements of different stakeholders. This understanding allows teams to effectively engage with stakeholders, gather feedback, prioritize features, and make informed decisions that align with the overall project goals and stakeholder interests. Ultimately, stakeholder personas help foster better collaboration, stakeholder satisfaction, and project success.
