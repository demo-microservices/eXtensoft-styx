Playbook Name:

Purpose & Discovery Playbook

Description:

This Playbook serves as a comprehensive guide to navigate the initial stage of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). It aims to establish a strong foundation for the project by focusing on defining its core objectives, identifying stakeholders, and mitigating initial risks.

Purpose:

The primary goal of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook" is to facilitate a smooth initiation into the SDLC. It provides structured yet flexible plays that guide the team in articulating the project's motivation, goals, and value proposition. It also aids in identifying crucial stakeholders, initial risks, compliance constraints, and gives an overall understanding of the domain and scope of the project.

Scope:

The scope of this Playbook is confined to the "Purpose & Discovery" phase of the SDLC. It covers a range of plays that each align with various perspectivesâ€”Business, Project, Technical, and Construction. Specific plays within this playbook include:

  • Domain Analysis Play: For understanding the project domain and its complexities.

  • Stakeholders Play: For identifying and engaging all parties invested in the project.

  • Product Backlog Play: For initial planning and prioritization of features.

  • Scope Play: For defining what is in scope and what is out of scope for the project.

  • System Analysis Play: For an initial analysis of existing systems that might be affected or might affect the new project.

  • Governance Play: To ensure that the project aligns with compliance and company policies.

  • Technology Play: To evaluate the technology stack and any constraints it may pose.

By following the guidelines set in these plays, stakeholders will have a structured yet flexible framework to ensure a successful kick-off to any software development project.

Play Name:

Domain Analysis Play

Purpose:

The primary purpose of the "Domain Analysis Play" is to thoroughly investigate, understand, and document the business or application domain that the project aims to serve. This forms the foundation upon which subsequent project phases will build, ensuring alignment with real-world constraints and needs.

Checklist:

  1. Identify Key Business Entities: List all business entities that will interact with the system.

  2. Collect Domain Documents: Collect any existing documentation or data models related to the business domain.

  3. Stakeholder Interviews: Conduct interviews with stakeholders to gather more nuanced insights into the domain.

  4. Study Existing Systems: Review any existing systems to understand current limitations and opportunities.

  5. Prepare Entity-Relationship Diagrams: Document relationships among key entities within the business domain.

  6. Understand Business Rules: Gather and document any rules or constraints that affect business entities or their relationships.

  7. Develop Use-Cases: Create use-case scenarios to explain how different entities interact with the system.

  8. Feedback Loop: Review the gathered information with stakeholders for validation.

Key Questions:

  • What are the core entities in the domain, and how do they interact?

  • Are there existing systems or processes that this project should align with or replace?

  • What are the known constraints or rules governing this domain?

  • What insights have been gathered from stakeholders, and how do they impact the project?

  • Have any domain-specific risks or assumptions been identified?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play concentrates specifically on understanding the domain, ensuring that team members and stakeholders have a solid and shared comprehension of the field in which they are operating.

  • Flexibility: While designed to be part of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook," this play can also stand alone to guide domain analysis in other contexts or projects.

  • Cohesion: By rigorously following this play, stakeholders will ensure that the resulting system or project is tightly aligned with the domain's actual needs and constraints, thereby contributing to the broader goals of the Playbook.

By adhering to the Domain Analysis Play, project teams can move forward with a shared, comprehensive understanding of the business or application domain, making future phases of the project more effective and efficient.

Play Name:

Stakeholders Play

Purpose:

The primary purpose of the "Stakeholders Play" is to identify, engage, and document all stakeholders involved in the project. This ensures that their needs, limitations, and roles are well-understood and incorporated into project planning and execution.

Checklist:

  1. Identify Stakeholders: List all individuals, teams, or organizations that have a vested interest in the project.

  2. Categorize Stakeholders: Classify stakeholders based on their impact and interest in the project (e.g., sponsors, users, regulatory bodies).

  3. Stakeholder Analysis: Perform a detailed analysis to understand each stakeholder's influence, needs, and communication requirements.

  4. Develop Communication Plan: Create a plan outlining how and when stakeholders will be engaged and informed throughout the project lifecycle.

  5. Stakeholder Interviews: Conduct interviews or surveys to gather stakeholder requirements, concerns, and expectations.

  6. Document Stakeholder Roles: Clearly outline roles and responsibilities for each stakeholder group.

  7. Secure Early Buy-In: Engage key stakeholders early to secure their buy-in and ensure smoother project progress.

  8. Feedback Loop: Maintain open lines of communication to regularly update stakeholders and incorporate their feedback.

Key Questions:

  • Who are the key stakeholders and what are their roles in this project?

  • What are the unique needs and constraints of each stakeholder group?

  • How will stakeholders be informed and engaged throughout the project?

  • Are there conflicting stakeholder interests, and if so, how will they be managed?

  • What mechanisms are in place for continuous stakeholder feedback and adjustment?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play specifically targets stakeholder engagement and documentation, ensuring that all involved parties are aligned and their needs are addressed.

  • Flexibility: While intended to be part of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook," this play can function independently to provide focused guidance on stakeholder management in various contexts.

  • Cohesion: Following this play ensures that stakeholder engagement is not just a checkbox activity but an integrated part of the project strategy, which in turn supports the broader goals of the Playbook.

By meticulously executing the Stakeholders Play, the project team can ensure that all human elements affecting the project are accounted for, which not only reduces risks but also improves the chances of project success.

Play Name:

Product Backlog Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "Product Backlog Play" is to create a prioritized list of features, user stories, and tasks that need to be addressed during the software development cycle. The aim is to align these items with stakeholder expectations, project goals, and timelines.

Checklist:

  1. Gather Requirements: Collect feature requests, user stories, and functional requirements from stakeholders and domain analysis.

  2. Prioritize Items: Use a prioritization method like MoSCoW or WSJF to rank backlog items based on their importance and impact.

  3. Estimation: Apply effort estimation techniques like Story Points or T-shirt sizes to each backlog item.

  4. Alignment with Objectives: Ensure that each backlog item is aligned with the project's overall objectives and value proposition.

  5. Dependency Analysis: Identify any dependencies between backlog items and plan for them.

  6. Backlog Refinement: Regularly update and refine the backlog to account for changes in requirements, estimates, or priorities.

  7. Stakeholder Review: Present the initial backlog to stakeholders for input and adjustment.

  8. Iteration Planning: Prepare for the first sprint/iteration by selecting a subset of high-priority, "ready" backlog items.

Key Questions:

  • What are the key features and requirements that stakeholders have identified?

  • How are backlog items prioritized and why?

  • What is the estimated effort required for each backlog item?

  • Are there any dependencies between backlog items that could impact their execution?

  • How often will the backlog be reviewed and updated?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: Provides targeted guidelines on developing and maintaining a product backlog, ensuring alignment with project objectives and stakeholder expectations.

  • Flexibility: While designed to fit within the broader "Purpose & Discovery Playbook," this play offers specific, actionable steps that can be applied in any software development context.

  • Cohesion: Adhering to the steps and key questions in this play ensures that the product backlog remains a dynamic, yet consistently aligned part of your overall project strategy.

Through effective execution of the Product Backlog Play, the team can better ensure that they are working on the most impactful features first, optimizing both time and resources. It provides a foundation for all future work and serves as a living document that adapts as the project progresses.

Play Name:

Scope Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "Scope Play" is to clearly define what is in and out of the project's scope, thereby setting accurate expectations among all stakeholders. By clearly outlining the project's boundaries, the play aims to manage stakeholder expectations and control project costs and timelines.

Checklist:

  1. Scope Identification: Gather all functional and non-functional requirements, features, and limitations that should be a part of the project.

  2. Stakeholder Validation: Validate scope items with key stakeholders to ensure alignment with business goals.

  3. Scope Documenting: Create a scope statement or scope document that outlines the boundaries of the project.

  4. Boundary Setting: Explicitly state what is outside the project's scope to prevent scope creep.

  5. Prioritization: Prioritize scope items in alignment with stakeholder needs and project goals.

  6. Dependency Analysis: Evaluate if any scope items are dependent on other projects or external factors.

  7. Approval: Obtain formal approval on the project scope from key stakeholders.

  8. Baseline Setting: Establish the approved scope as the baseline for project execution and change control.

Key Questions:

  • What are the essential features and functions that must be part of the project?

  • Who are the key stakeholders that need to validate the scope?

  • Are there any gray areas that could become potential scope creep?

  • How does this scope align with organizational goals and compliance requirements?

  • Is the scope realistic within the projectâ€™s time and budget constraints?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play helps to concentrate efforts on what is truly important for the project's success, ensuring that all stakeholders have a shared understanding of what the project will deliver.

  • Flexibility: Designed to provide in-depth, actionable steps for defining a project's scope, this play can also stand alone as a reference guide for scope management in general.

  • Cohesion: Following this play ensures that the project's scope remains consistent and approved, which in turn helps in achieving the broader goals set in the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook."

Understanding and executing the Scope Play ensures that everyone involved knows what to expect from the project, thus mitigating the risks associated with scope creep, budget overruns, and stakeholder dissatisfaction.

System Analysis Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "System Analysis Play" is to understand the technical landscape surrounding the project. This includes evaluating current systems, identifying gaps, and understanding how the new or modified system will fit into the existing architecture.

Checklist:

  1. Current System Evaluation: Conduct a thorough analysis of the existing systems, data flows, and technological stack.

  2. Gap Identification: Identify functional and technical gaps in the current system that the new project aims to fill.

  3. Interoperability Assessment: Assess how the new system will interact with existing systems, services, and data stores.

  4. Technology Stack Decision: Based on analysis, finalize the technology stack that will be used in the project.

  5. Compliance Check: Ensure that the chosen technologies and designs comply with industry regulations and organizational policies.

  6. Documentation: Create or update system architecture diagrams, data models, and other relevant documentation.

  7. Stakeholder Review: Share findings and recommendations with stakeholders for validation.

  8. Revision and Approval: Make any necessary revisions and obtain formal approvals.

Key Questions:

  • What are the current systems in place, and how do they interact with each other?

  • What gaps or inefficiencies exist in the current system landscape?

  • How will the new system integrate with or replace the existing ones?

  • Are there any compliance or governance issues to be considered?

  • What technologies are most suitable for addressing the identified needs and gaps?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play provides a roadmap for analyzing the existing systems, ensuring the project is well-integrated into the existing technological landscape.

  • Flexibility: While contributing to the projectâ€™s overall strategy, this play can also be a stand-alone guide for system analysis in other contexts.

  • Cohesion: Adhering to the System Analysis Play ensures that the project fits within the broader ecosystem, aligning with the aims of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook."

Executing the System Analysis Play enables teams to make informed decisions, reducing the risks associated with system integration, scalability, and compliance. It ensures that the new system will not only meet the functional requirements but also be a well-fitted piece in the existing technology puzzle.

Governance Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "Governance Play" is to establish the rules, policies, and decision-making frameworks that will guide the project. This includes identifying relevant regulations, ensuring compliance, and establishing clear roles and responsibilities for governance oversight.

Checklist:

  1. Identify Governance Bodies: Establish which internal and external governance bodies have oversight for this project.

  2. Regulatory Landscape: Understand all laws, regulations, and corporate policies that may affect the project.

  3. Roles and Responsibilities: Clearly define who is responsible for governance-related tasks and decision-making.

  4. Compliance Mechanisms: Implement tools and practices to ensure ongoing compliance with regulations and policies.

  5. Decision Framework: Create a decision-making process that considers risk, compliance, and business value.

  6. Documentation and Record-keeping: Document all governance activities, decisions, and compliance checks.

  7. Stakeholder Communication: Ensure that all stakeholders are aware of governance processes and outcomes.

  8. Audit Preparedness: Establish routines for internal and external audits to assess governance and compliance.

  9. Review and Update: Regularly review the governance framework and make updates as necessary.

Key Questions:

  • What are the regulatory requirements affecting this project?

  • Who has the ultimate authority for various types of project decisions?

  • How will compliance be monitored and maintained?

  • What are the consequences of non-compliance?

  • How will governance activities be documented and communicated?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: The play provides a focused approach to governance, ensuring that the project aligns with legal, ethical, and corporate frameworks.

  • Flexibility: This play can be adapted for different projects and organizational needs, serving as a modular guide for governance.

  • Cohesion: By following the Governance Play, all stakeholders will operate under a unified framework, contributing to the broader objectives of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook."

The Governance Play serves to mitigate the risks associated with legal non-compliance, misalignment with organizational policies, and inadequate oversight. It is crucial for establishing a robust governance framework that ensures long-term project sustainability and integrity.

Technology Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "Technology Play" is to analyze, evaluate, and select the appropriate technology stack and tools for the project. This ensures that the technology used aligns with the project's goals, scalability requirements, and complies with organizational standards.

Checklist:

  1. Technology Stack Assessment: Evaluate various technology stacks based on project needs and goals.

  2. Compatibility Check: Ensure that the chosen technology is compatible with existing systems and future scalability.

  3. Governance and Compliance: Verify that the chosen technologies align with governance policies and compliance requirements.

  4. Cost-Benefit Analysis: Perform a cost-benefit analysis to ensure that the technology provides value for the investment.

  5. Security Review: Conduct a security assessment to identify any potential vulnerabilities.

  6. Team Expertise: Consider the skillset of the development team and how well it aligns with the chosen technology.

  7. Pilot Testing: Run a small-scale test to validate the technology choices before full-scale implementation.

  8. Documentation: Document the technology choices, reasons for selection, and any potential risks.

  9. Stakeholder Communication: Ensure that key stakeholders are informed and in agreement with the technology choices made.

Key Questions:

  • What are the technology requirements for this project?

  • How does the chosen technology align with organizational and project goals?

  • Are there any governance or compliance issues related to the technology selection?

  • What are the costs involved, and do they justify the benefits?

  • Are there any security vulnerabilities to be aware of?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play hones in on the critical technology-related aspects of the project, providing clear directives to align technology choices with project objectives.

  • Flexibility: It offers a modular guide to technology selection that can be adapted depending on project or organizational needs.

  • Cohesion: By adhering to the steps and insights of this play, stakeholders ensure technology decisions are cohesive with the broader objectives outlined in the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook."

The Technology Play is essential for making informed, effective decisions about technology use in the project. It serves as a robust guide that reduces risks related to technology choices, ensuring alignment with both business goals and governance frameworks.
