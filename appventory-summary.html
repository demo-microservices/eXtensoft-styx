1. Size & Scope of Application

Why it Matters: The larger the scope and user base of an application, the more complex and resource-intensive the replacement might be. On the other hand, retiring small, rarely-used applications can yield quick cost savings and reduce IT complexity.

2. Business Criticality of Application

Why it Matters: Applications pivotal to business operations need careful planning during retirement to ensure there's minimal disruption. Their replacement should ideally bring enhanced value to users and the business.

3. Basic Nature of the System

Why it Matters: Understanding the foundational nature helps in deciding what kind of system can replace it. For instance, a legacy reporting system might be replaced with a modern BI tool, or an old CMS might be superseded by a cloud-based solution.

4. Operational Health & Efficiency

Why it Matters: An application riddled with issues or inefficiencies is a prime candidate for replacement. Conversely, applications with high operational health might just need minor upgrades instead of a complete overhaul.

5. Adjacent Dependencies

Why it Matters: Retiring an application can impact other systems dependent on it. It's crucial to map and understand these dependencies to avoid cascading failures or disruptions.

6. Financial Perspective

Why it Matters: Financial considerations often drive retirement decisions. If maintaining an old application is cost-prohibitive or its ROI is dwindling, it might be economically wise to replace it with a more cost-effective solution.

7. Lifecycle Perspective

Why it Matters: An application nearing its end-of-life, with outdated technologies and no future enhancements, poses risks. It can become incompatible with newer systems, lack vendor support, and become a security liability.

8. Regulatory & Compliance Perspective

Why it Matters: As regulations evolve, applications need to stay compliant. An outdated application might fall short of current regulations, making it essential to replace to avoid legal repercussions and ensure data protection.

This angle helps prioritize which applications to target first and aids in strategizing the "how" and "when" aspects of the Retire & Replace process.
