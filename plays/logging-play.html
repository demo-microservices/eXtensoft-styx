Logging for Visibility and Forensics Play

Name & Purpose:
Logging for Visibility and Forensics Play - To ensure that application logging is implemented in a manner that facilitates both real-time visibility into system operations and the forensic analysis of past events.

Checklist:

  1. Identify the logging needs and standards for different components of the application.
  2. Determine the level of granularity for logs (Debug, Info, Warning, Error).
  3. Choose the logging tools and storage solutions that align with project requirements.
  4. Configure log rotation and archival policies.
  5. Implement semantic logging practices for better searchability and analytics.
  6. Verify that sensitive information is not being logged inadvertently.
  7. Validate the effectiveness of logging through periodic reviews and simulated incidents.

Key Questions:

  1. What are the primary objectives for application logging in this project?
  2. How are the logs secured to ensure they don’t become a vulnerability?
  3. What is the plan for log analysis and alerting?
  4. How does logging integrate with other Plays in the Cross-Cutting Concerns Playbook?
  5. Are there regulatory compliance requirements affecting logging, and if so, how are these addressed?
  6. How is log storage managed to ensure availability and scalability?
  7. What strategies are in place for real-time monitoring and historical analysis?

The Logging for Visibility and Forensics Play is a critical part of the Cross-Cutting Concerns Playbook as it helps teams establish a foundation for transparency, troubleshooting, and compliance. It's a comprehensive guide that covers best practices for semantic logging that facilitates both real-time system monitoring and forensic investigations. By adhering to the steps and insights of this Play, stakeholders can ensure that their logging strategy aligns with broader project goals and compliance requirements.
