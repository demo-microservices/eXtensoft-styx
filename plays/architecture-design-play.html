Architecture & Design Play

Name & Purpose:

Name: Architecture & Design Play
Purpose: To ensure that the architecture and design of the software or system align with technical feasibility, non-functional requirements, domain Entity/Relationship (E/R) analysis, and expected business growth.

Checklist:

  1. Conduct a technical feasibility study to assess resource allocation, technical stack, and infrastructure requirements.
  2. Evaluate and validate non-functional requirements such as performance, scalability, and security.
  3. Conduct domain E/R analysis to align the software design with the business model.
  4. Project expected business growth and consider scalability and extensibility in the design.
  5. Get architectural and design decisions peer-reviewed and approved by appropriate stakeholders.

Key Questions:

  1. Does the architecture align with the identified non-functional requirements?
  2. How does the design map to the domain E/R analysis?
  3. Are there contingencies in the design to accommodate expected business growth?
  4. How are technical risks and constraints addressed in the architecture?
  5. Who are the key stakeholders that should review and approve the architecture and design?

The Architecture & Design Play aims to keep stakeholders focused on aligning the software's architecture and design with the project's broader objectives and constraints. By diligently following the checklist and answering the key questions, stakeholders can ensure that the architecture and design are both feasible and in sync with business and technical requirements. This play offers flexibility in its approach, allowing teams to adapt to specific challenges they may face while ensuring cohesion with the playbook's overarching goals.
