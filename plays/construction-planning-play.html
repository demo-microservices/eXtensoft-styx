Construction Planning Play

Name & Purpose:

Name: Construction Planning Play
Purpose: To meticulously plan and structure the construction phase of the software development cycle, including setting up sprints with milestones and deliverables, and establishing quality assurance controls and testing procedures.

Checklist:

  1. Identify the set of features or components to be implemented during the construction phase.
  2. Break down the features or components into manageable tasks and group them into sprints.
  3. Set clear milestones and deliverables for each sprint.
  4. Establish quality assurance controls, including coding guidelines, test coverage expectations, and code review strategies.
  5. Create a risk mitigation plan to address potential issues that may arise during the construction phase.

Key Questions:

  1. What features or components are targeted for implementation in this construction phase?
  2. How are the sprints structured, and what are the associated milestones and deliverables?
  3. What quality assurance measures, like code reviews and testing, have been put in place?
  4. Are there any specific risks anticipated during the construction phase, and how are they being mitigated?
  5. How will progress be tracked and reported to stakeholders?

The Construction Planning Play aims to provide a structured framework for organizing and executing the construction phase of the project. It guides stakeholders in setting up effective sprints and implementing robust quality assurance measures. This play is designed to stand alone but is most effective when used in conjunction with other plays in the Planning & Risk Mitigation Playbook. Its key utility lies in focusing attention on planning details that are critical to a successful construction phase, ensuring alignment with the broader objectives of the project.
