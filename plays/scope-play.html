Play Name:

Scope Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "Scope Play" is to clearly define what is in and out of the project's scope, thereby setting accurate expectations among all stakeholders. By clearly outlining the project's boundaries, the play aims to manage stakeholder expectations and control project costs and timelines.

Checklist:

  1. Scope Identification: Gather all functional and non-functional requirements, features, and limitations that should be a part of the project.

  2. Stakeholder Validation: Validate scope items with key stakeholders to ensure alignment with business goals.

  3. Scope Documenting: Create a scope statement or scope document that outlines the boundaries of the project.

  4. Boundary Setting: Explicitly state what is outside the project's scope to prevent scope creep.

  5. Prioritization: Prioritize scope items in alignment with stakeholder needs and project goals.

  6. Dependency Analysis: Evaluate if any scope items are dependent on other projects or external factors.

  7. Approval: Obtain formal approval on the project scope from key stakeholders.

  8. Baseline Setting: Establish the approved scope as the baseline for project execution and change control.

Key Questions:

  • What are the essential features and functions that must be part of the project?

  • Who are the key stakeholders that need to validate the scope?

  • Are there any gray areas that could become potential scope creep?

  • How does this scope align with organizational goals and compliance requirements?

  • Is the scope realistic within the projectâ€™s time and budget constraints?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play helps to concentrate efforts on what is truly important for the project's success, ensuring that all stakeholders have a shared understanding of what the project will deliver.

  • Flexibility: Designed to provide in-depth, actionable steps for defining a project's scope, this play can also stand alone as a reference guide for scope management in general.

  • Cohesion: Following this play ensures that the project's scope remains consistent and approved, which in turn helps in achieving the broader goals set in the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook."

Understanding and executing the Scope Play ensures that everyone involved knows what to expect from the project, thus mitigating the risks associated with scope creep, budget overruns, and stakeholder dissatisfaction.
