Play Name:

Stakeholders Play

Purpose:

The primary purpose of the "Stakeholders Play" is to identify, engage, and document all stakeholders involved in the project. This ensures that their needs, limitations, and roles are well-understood and incorporated into project planning and execution.

Checklist:

  1. Identify Stakeholders: List all individuals, teams, or organizations that have a vested interest in the project.

  2. Categorize Stakeholders: Classify stakeholders based on their impact and interest in the project (e.g., sponsors, users, regulatory bodies).

  3. Stakeholder Analysis: Perform a detailed analysis to understand each stakeholder's influence, needs, and communication requirements.

  4. Develop Communication Plan: Create a plan outlining how and when stakeholders will be engaged and informed throughout the project lifecycle.

  5. Stakeholder Interviews: Conduct interviews or surveys to gather stakeholder requirements, concerns, and expectations.

  6. Document Stakeholder Roles: Clearly outline roles and responsibilities for each stakeholder group.

  7. Secure Early Buy-In: Engage key stakeholders early to secure their buy-in and ensure smoother project progress.

  8. Feedback Loop: Maintain open lines of communication to regularly update stakeholders and incorporate their feedback.

Key Questions:

  • Who are the key stakeholders and what are their roles in this project?

  • What are the unique needs and constraints of each stakeholder group?

  • How will stakeholders be informed and engaged throughout the project?

  • Are there conflicting stakeholder interests, and if so, how will they be managed?

  • What mechanisms are in place for continuous stakeholder feedback and adjustment?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play specifically targets stakeholder engagement and documentation, ensuring that all involved parties are aligned and their needs are addressed.

  • Flexibility: While intended to be part of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook," this play can function independently to provide focused guidance on stakeholder management in various contexts.

  • Cohesion: Following this play ensures that stakeholder engagement is not just a checkbox activity but an integrated part of the project strategy, which in turn supports the broader goals of the Playbook.

By meticulously executing the Stakeholders Play, the project team can ensure that all human elements affecting the project are accounted for, which not only reduces risks but also improves the chances of project success.
