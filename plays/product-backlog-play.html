Play Name:

Product Backlog Play

Purpose:

The purpose of the "Product Backlog Play" is to create a prioritized list of features, user stories, and tasks that need to be addressed during the software development cycle. The aim is to align these items with stakeholder expectations, project goals, and timelines.

Checklist:

  1. Gather Requirements: Collect feature requests, user stories, and functional requirements from stakeholders and domain analysis.

  2. Prioritize Items: Use a prioritization method like MoSCoW or WSJF to rank backlog items based on their importance and impact.

  3. Estimation: Apply effort estimation techniques like Story Points or T-shirt sizes to each backlog item.

  4. Alignment with Objectives: Ensure that each backlog item is aligned with the project's overall objectives and value proposition.

  5. Dependency Analysis: Identify any dependencies between backlog items and plan for them.

  6. Backlog Refinement: Regularly update and refine the backlog to account for changes in requirements, estimates, or priorities.

  7. Stakeholder Review: Present the initial backlog to stakeholders for input and adjustment.

  8. Iteration Planning: Prepare for the first sprint/iteration by selecting a subset of high-priority, "ready" backlog items.

Key Questions:

  • What are the key features and requirements that stakeholders have identified?

  • How are backlog items prioritized and why?

  • What is the estimated effort required for each backlog item?

  • Are there any dependencies between backlog items that could impact their execution?

  • How often will the backlog be reviewed and updated?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: Provides targeted guidelines on developing and maintaining a product backlog, ensuring alignment with project objectives and stakeholder expectations.

  • Flexibility: While designed to fit within the broader "Purpose & Discovery Playbook," this play offers specific, actionable steps that can be applied in any software development context.

  • Cohesion: Adhering to the steps and key questions in this play ensures that the product backlog remains a dynamic, yet consistently aligned part of your overall project strategy.

Through effective execution of the Product Backlog Play, the team can better ensure that they are working on the most impactful features first, optimizing both time and resources. It provides a foundation for all future work and serves as a living document that adapts as the project progresses.
