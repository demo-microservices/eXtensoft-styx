Delivery Planning Play

Name & Purpose:

Name: Delivery Planning Play
Purpose: To outline and streamline the deployment process, ensuring that the software is delivered efficiently and securely to the target environment. This play focuses on the stages of delivery, pipeline management, and configuration and secrets management.

Checklist:

  1. Define the stages involved in the software delivery process, from code completion to deployment.
  2. Design and document the deployment pipeline, including build, test, and release stages.
  3. Identify tools and platforms to be used for continuous integration and continuous deployment (CI/CD).
  4. Set up configuration and secrets management protocols to ensure secure delivery.
  5. Develop a rollback plan in case of unsuccessful deployment.

Key Questions:

  1. What are the critical stages in the delivery process, and what steps are involved in each stage?
  2. How is the deployment pipeline structured for efficient and reliable delivery?
  3. What CI/CD tools are in use, and how do they align with the overall delivery objectives?
  4. How is configuration and secrets management handled to ensure secure deployment?
  5. What is the contingency plan in case the deployment process fails or needs to be rolled back?

The Delivery Planning Play serves to concentrate efforts on the often-overlooked, but critical, aspects of deploying software to its operational environment. By paying attention to the details of the deployment pipeline and the stages involved in the delivery, this play ensures that the software is delivered efficiently, reliably, and securely. While designed to be a standalone guide, this play is most effective when executed as a part of the Planning & Risk Mitigation Playbook, contributing to a cohesive and comprehensive planning strategy.
