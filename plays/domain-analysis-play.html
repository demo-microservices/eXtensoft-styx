Play Name:

Domain Analysis Play

Purpose:

The primary purpose of the "Domain Analysis Play" is to thoroughly investigate, understand, and document the business or application domain that the project aims to serve. This forms the foundation upon which subsequent project phases will build, ensuring alignment with real-world constraints and needs.

Checklist:

  1. Identify Key Business Entities: List all business entities that will interact with the system.

  2. Collect Domain Documents: Collect any existing documentation or data models related to the business domain.

  3. Stakeholder Interviews: Conduct interviews with stakeholders to gather more nuanced insights into the domain.

  4. Study Existing Systems: Review any existing systems to understand current limitations and opportunities.

  5. Prepare Entity-Relationship Diagrams: Document relationships among key entities within the business domain.

  6. Understand Business Rules: Gather and document any rules or constraints that affect business entities or their relationships.

  7. Develop Use-Cases: Create use-case scenarios to explain how different entities interact with the system.

  8. Feedback Loop: Review the gathered information with stakeholders for validation.

Key Questions:

  • What are the core entities in the domain, and how do they interact?

  • Are there existing systems or processes that this project should align with or replace?

  • What are the known constraints or rules governing this domain?

  • What insights have been gathered from stakeholders, and how do they impact the project?

  • Have any domain-specific risks or assumptions been identified?

Primary Utility:

  • Focus: This play concentrates specifically on understanding the domain, ensuring that team members and stakeholders have a solid and shared comprehension of the field in which they are operating.

  • Flexibility: While designed to be part of the "Purpose & Discovery Playbook," this play can also stand alone to guide domain analysis in other contexts or projects.

  • Cohesion: By rigorously following this play, stakeholders will ensure that the resulting system or project is tightly aligned with the domain's actual needs and constraints, thereby contributing to the broader goals of the Playbook.

By adhering to the Domain Analysis Play, project teams can move forward with a shared, comprehensive understanding of the business or application domain, making future phases of the project more effective and efficient.
