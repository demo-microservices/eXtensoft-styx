Business Perspective:

  • Scope: Defining what's in and out for the project.
  • Requirements: Capturing the detailed needs of the stakeholders.
  • Functional Requirements: Specific behaviors or functions of the system.
  • User Stories/Use Cases: Narrative descriptions of functionalities from an end-user perspective.
  • Personas: Representations of the key audience segments.
  • Expected Business Growth: Anticipating and planning for scalability and future needs.
  • Estimating Size & Cost: Gauging the scale and anticipated expenses of the project.
  • Funding: Securing and managing the financial resources necessary for the project.
  • Prioritized Backlog: Organizing tasks and features based on their importance, value, and impact on the business.
  • Business Criticality Assessment: Evaluating the importance and urgency of different components or features to the business operations.
  • ROI Analysis: Predicting and measuring the return on investment for the development effort.

Technical Perspective:

  • Business Domain Analysis: Comprehensive understanding of the business's functional areas.
  • Dataflows: Mapping the movement of data within and across systems.
  • Adjacent Systems: Recognizing interconnected systems and their dependencies.
  • SLA & Design Alignment: Setting and meeting service level agreements and performance benchmarks.
  • Quality Assurance: Ensuring the product meets desired standards.
  • Security: Incorporating security principles throughout the development and operational phases.
  • Delivery & Configuration Management: Emphasizing automation, continuous integration, and continuous delivery.

Project Perspective:

  • Stakeholder Involvement: Ensuring active participation and feedback.
  • Resource Allocation: Ensuring proper personnel, time, and budget.
  • Risk Management: Identifying and addressing potential issues.
  • Communication: Maintaining clear lines of dialogue among all involved.
  • Documentation: Keeping records of decisions, designs, and changes.
  • Feedback Loops: Regular check-ins to ensure alignment with objectives.

Regulatory & Oversight Perspective:

  • Mandates/Compliance/Governance: Meeting required standards, regulations, and practices (e.g., Section 508, PII management, and more).
