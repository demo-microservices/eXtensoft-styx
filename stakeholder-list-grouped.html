Stakeholders

1. Business

  • Product Owner: The individual responsible for defining and prioritizing the product backlog, ensuring the team is working on tasks that deliver maximum business value.
  • Product Manager: Manages the product lifecycle, bridges the gap between technical and non-technical teams, and ensures the product aligns with business goals.
  • Business Analyst: Analyzes business processes and works closely with the product owner to define requirements for system changes or enhancements.
  • Subject Matter Expert: Person with comprehensive and authoritative knowledge in a specific area.
  • Users: The end-users who interact with the application or system.

2. Project

  • Project Manager: Responsible for planning, executing, and closing projects, ensuring they are completed on time and within scope and budget.
  • Facilitator/Scrum Master: Ensures the agile team follows agile practices, removes impediments, and ensures effective communication within the team.

3. Enterprise Architecture

  • Enterprise Architect: Provides an overall vision for the enterprise's strategy and ensures alignment between the business's needs and IT architecture.
  • Solution Architect: Plans, designs, and manages the solution architecture for specific business problems.

4. Technical

  • Domain Analyst: Specialist in analyzing a specific business domain, understanding its intricacies, and translating them into technical requirements.
  • Architect/Technical Lead: Guides the technical team, ensures the technical solution aligns with architectural standards and addresses business needs.
  • Application Developer: Writes code and builds the application or system based on requirements.
  • Devops Engineer: Integrates development and operations, focusing on automating the software delivery process.
  • Database Admin: Manages and maintains the database systems, ensuring data consistency, integrity, and availability.
  • QA Tester: Ensures the quality of the application through systematic testing.
  • UAT Tester: End-users who validate the solution in the User Acceptance Testing phase.
  • Development Manager: Leads the development team, ensuring they have the tools, resources, and processes to deliver the application efficiently.

This structured approach provides a clear overview of the roles and responsibilities of each stakeholder in the context of the project.
